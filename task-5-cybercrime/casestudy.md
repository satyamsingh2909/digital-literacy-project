# Case Study: The Growing Threat of Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a prevalent cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted organizations—such as banks, universities, or government agencies—to deceive individuals into revealing sensitive data like passwords, UPI PINs, or credit card numbers.

This crime typically unfolds in a four-step process: First, the attacker sends a deceptive message (email or SMS) designed to trigger fear or urgency, such as a warning that a bank account will be suspended. Second, the panicked victim clicks a provided link, which directs them to a sophisticated, fake website mimicking the real institution. Third, the victim unknowingly enters their login credentials or financial details into the fake site. Finally, the attacker harvests this information to drain accounts or commit identity theft.

While anyone can be targeted, college students and young professionals are increasingly frequent victims due to their heavy reliance on digital payment apps and university online portals. The consequences are severe, ranging from devastating financial losses to compromised digital identities and significant psychological distress.

For example, in a major 2022 corporate breach, a multinational bank suffered massive financial and reputational damage simply because an employee clicked a fraudulent verification link. This highlights that phishing exploits human psychology rather than technical flaws, proving that a single click can lead to catastrophic data loss for both individuals and organizations.
